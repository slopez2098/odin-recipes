Churros

Description

A fired dough desert topped with sugar and cinnamon. This dish originates from Mexico but may be found in several other Latin American courties. This dish is usually served on its own but at times may be paired with a chocolate dipping sauce like the ones we're making today. The recipe itself yields around 16 churros in total.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of water
  • 6 tbsp of butter
  • 2 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Cinnamon Sugar
  • 3/4 cup chocolate chips
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp of more salt (for the chocolate sauce

Steps

  1. To make the churros, we begin by adding water, butter and sugar in a large saucepan over medium heat. Bring the mixture to a boil, then add the vanilla extract.
  2. Turn off the heat and add flour and salt. Stir mixture until thickened (around 30 seconds). Let the mixture cool for 10 minutes.
  3. Once cooled, using a hand mixer, beat the eggs in one at a time time everything is combined. Then transfer the mixture to a piping page with a large star tip.
  4. In a large pot add in oil until halfway up the sides and heat to 375 degrees F over medium heat.
  5. Holding the piping bag a few inched above the oil, carefully pipe in 6" long ropes, using scissors to cut off each strand.
  6. Fry until golden, usually taking 4-5 minutes per churro. After each batch (3-4 churros per batch) let oil go back to 375 degrees F.
  7. Remove the churros with slotted spoon or tongs and immediately roll into cinnamon sugar. Then place the coated churros on a cooling rack.
  8. To make the chocolate sauce begin by placing the chcolate chips in a medium heatproof bowl. In another small saucepan bring heavy cream to a simmer over medium heat, then pour hot cream over chocolate chips and let sit for 2 minutes. Add the cinnamon and salt and whisk to combine.
  9. Serve churros and chocolate sauce and enjoy.
